# Product Requirements Document And Strategy

## 1. Executive Summary

Thailand Sensory Intelligence Platform is a national sensory knowledge and deployment system that archives Thai scent identity, converts cultural and institutional context into scent strategies, measures public experience impact, and creates new economic value through consulting, licensing, research, government projects, and international expansion.

The platform is inspired by Karmakamet's strength in scent, memory, craft, and Thai emotional storytelling, but it expands the idea into a public-private infrastructure for cities, institutions, tourism, wellness, education, and cultural diplomacy.

## 2. Vision

To make Thailand the regional leader in sensory intelligence by transforming scent into a measurable language of trust, place identity, public experience, wellness, and soft power.

## 3. Product Principles

- Culture first: every scent must connect to Thai place, material, memory, ritual, or civic meaning.
- Research-backed: all deployment must reference scent science, psychology, ethnography, or evaluated case studies.
- Measurable experience: scent is treated as an intervention with baseline, deployment, and post-implementation measurement.
- Institutional dignity: public-sector scents should support trust, calm, clarity, and service quality, not decoration.
- Ethical sensory design: no hidden manipulation, no medical claims without evidence, and clear governance for consent-sensitive environments.

## 8. Phase 5: Pilot Project

### Police Station Signature Scent: Scent Of Trust

**Objective:** increase trust, improve public experience, and strengthen institutional identity.

**Pilot scope:** 3 police stations across different service contexts: urban high-volume, tourist-facing, and community station.

**Target zones:** reception, waiting area, citizen service counter, community meeting room.

**Excluded zones:** interrogation rooms, holding areas, emergency response areas, evidence rooms, and any zone flagged by legal or health review.

**KPIs:**

- Satisfaction Score
- Trust Score
- Recall Score
- Employee Engagement
- Complaint Frequency
- Waiting Experience Score
- Staff Acceptance

**Pilot method:**

1. Baseline survey for 2 weeks.
2. Scent deployment for 8 weeks.
3. Weekly intensity and operational checks.
4. Midpoint survey and staff interview.
5. Final survey and comparative analysis.
6. Executive report with rollout recommendation.

## 14. Governance Model

### Governance Bodies

- National Sensory Intelligence Council: strategic oversight, ethics, public-sector alignment.
- Research And Evidence Board: methodology, citation quality, research grading.
- Cultural Integrity Panel: cultural accuracy, community representation, non-extractive storytelling.
- Scent Safety And Deployment Review: allergen risk, intensity policy, sensitive environments.
- Product And Data Steering Group: platform roadmap, data quality, AI governance.

### Data Governance

- Every scent record has owner, source, evidence grade, and review status.
- Cultural stories must cite source type: field interview, archive, publication, institutional partner, or expert review.
- AI-generated content must be labeled and human-reviewed before publication.
- Sensitive institutional data is private by default.
