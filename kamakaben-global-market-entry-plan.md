# Kamakaben Global Market Entry Plan

## Market Thesis

Kamakaben can enter the global market through cultural experience before mass retail. The strongest first wedge is not "another fragrance product" but a Thai scent learning and ritual experience for partners that already host culturally curious audiences.

## Best First Markets

### 1. Thailand Premium Hospitality

Why:

- Immediate cultural fit
- Guests already seek Thai memory
- Hotels and spas understand scent value
- Easier pilot execution

Entry:

- Signature welcome scent
- In-room scent cards
- Gift sets
- QR learning page

### 2. Japan

Why:

- High appreciation for ritual, craft, packaging, seasonality, and subtle fragrance
- Strong cultural retail and concept store ecosystem

Entry:

- Discovery kit
- Scent card storytelling
- Gallery/cafe pop-up

### 3. Singapore

Why:

- Regional premium retail hub
- Strong hotel, airport, and cultural institution network
- English-first bridge to global audiences

Entry:

- Hotel lounge pilot
- Museum shop or concept store
- Corporate/cultural gifting

### 4. UK

Why:

- Strong creative industries and cultural institution network
- Good fit for Thai soft power and cultural learning positioning

Entry:

- Gallery collaboration
- Design week pop-up
- Press and concept store pitch

### 5. Middle East Luxury Hospitality

Why:

- High fragrance culture maturity
- Luxury hotels and gifting are strong channels

Entry:

- Premium Thai scent ritual
- High-end gift set
- Hotel signature scent pilot

## Go-To-Market Ladder

### Step 1: Brand Entity

- Landing page
- SEO metadata
- Brand profile PDF
- Scent card pages
- Access cards

### Step 2: Partner Pilot

- Choose 3 partner targets
- Offer one small pilot
- Capture guest/visitor reaction
- Produce case study

### Step 3: Proof Of Culture

- Publish reflection archive
- Build review and UGC system
- Collect press and partner quotes

### Step 4: Productization

- Discovery kit
- Gift set
- Partner edition
- Retail-ready packaging

### Step 5: International Activation

- Pop-up
- Design/culture fair
- Hotel/gallery collaboration
- Tourism/city branding partnership

## 90-Day Execution

### Days 1-14

- Confirm spelling, domain, social handles
- Deploy Kamakaben landing page
- Prepare 5 scent samples or scent concepts
- Generate production QR codes
- Finalize partner PDF

### Days 15-30

- Pitch 20 partners
- Book 5 discovery calls
- Select 1-2 pilots
- Prepare scent cards and feedback form

### Days 31-60

- Run first pilot
- Collect QR scans, feedback, photos, quotes
- Improve scent story and ritual copy
- Build first case study

### Days 61-90

- Launch second partner pilot
- Publish case study
- Send press outreach
- Prepare international market test kit

## Metrics

- Branded search visibility
- Social handle ownership
- Partner replies
- Discovery calls booked
- Pilot conversions
- QR scans
- Reflection submissions
- Gift set interest
- Press responses

## Risks

- Weak product proof if scent samples are not ready
- Cultural positioning can feel vague if not tied to real materials and places
- QR journey may fail if production URLs are not deployed
- Partner pitch needs strong visuals and physical samples
